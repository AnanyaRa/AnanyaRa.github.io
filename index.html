Overview

Welcome! I'm a first-year Ph.D. student in the Genomics and Computational Biology program at the University of Pennsylvania Perelman School of Medicine, with a focus on Biomedical Informatics. received my B.S. in Computer Science from Yale, where I also completed certificates in Global Health and Statistics. At Yale, I worked with Dr. Declan Clarke in the Gerstein Lab to analyze cell-type-specific gene expression in brain tissue.

I'm broadly interested in applying machine learning—both traditional and graph-based methods—to clinical, genomic, and environmental data to advance disease prediction and prevention in women's health. My first rotation was with Dr. Shefali Setia-Verma, where I studied the mediating role of polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS) in genetic risk for type 2 diabetes using data from the All of Us Research Program. My second rotation, with Dr. Joseph Romano, focused on long-term health outcomes following preeclampsia using Penn Medicine BioBank data. I am currently completing my third rotation with Dr. Sunni Mumford, where I'm working on updating a predictive model for in-vitro fertilization (IVF) success among individuals pursuing assisted reproductive therapy
