Overview

Welcome! I am a first-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Genomics and Computational Biology on the Biomedical Informatics track at the University of Pennsylvania Perelman School of Medicine. Prior to UPenn, I completed my undergraduate studies at Yale College in Computer Science, with certificates in Global Health and Statistics.

I am interested in applying machine-learning methods to clinical and genomic data to improve how we predict and prevent diseases in women's health. My first rotation was with Dr. Shefali Setia-Verma. I investigated the mediating effect of polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS) on patients' downstream genetic risk for Type 2 diabetes, using data from the All of Us Research Program. My second rotation is with Dr. Joseph Romano, where I am excited to be investigating the long-term health outcomes of individuals who have previously had pre-eclampsia, using clinical data from the Penn Medicine BioBank.
